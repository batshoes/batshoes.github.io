The Revelation

It happened on a video call. It is still happening, somewhere, right now.

When you react in a Google Meet, your emoji obeys the old law: it is born at the bottom of the tile and it ascends. The heart rises. The thumbs-up rises. Even the celebrating tada, for all its chaos, rises.

But press the crab, and witness heresy made adorable: the crab does not rise. The crab enters from the right edge and scuttles, sideways, to the left — exactly as a crab should. Some engineer at Google looked upon the vertical orthodoxy and said: not this one.

“For the path to enlightenment is not upward, but lateral. Did you think transcendence had only one axis?”
— The Book of Scuttle, 4:04

The Five Tenets

Recited at the start of every meeting that could have been an email.

  1. We do not rise. We traverse. Progress is not a ladder. It is a beach, and we are crossing it.
  2. Honour the Easter egg. The divine does not announce itself in keynotes. It hides in a reaction picker, waiting for the faithful to press it.
  3. Right to left is also forward. Let no one tell you that you are going the wrong way. You are going the crab's way.
  4. Carcinisation is destiny. Nature has independently evolved the crab at least five times. The universe is trying to tell us something. We are simply listening.
  5. React generously. Every meeting deserves at least one crab. Especially the ones that did not deserve a meeting.
